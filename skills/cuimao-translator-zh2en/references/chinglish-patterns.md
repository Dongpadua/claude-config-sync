# Chinglish Patterns (Chinese → English)

Common Chinese→English translation problems with real examples and fixes. Updated from actual translation critiques.

---

## 1. Fragment Sentences

Chinese often drops sentence connectors; English readers feel the gap.

❌ More than the way you look — the way you make me feel.
✅ It is not just your appearance, but the way you make me feel.
✅ It is not your beauty, but the feeling you bring into my life.

**Rule:** If the Chinese line starts a new idea without an explicit verb, provide one in English. Chinese 流水句 can stand alone; English fragments feel broken.

---

## 2. Direct Image Transfer Fails

Chinese poetic images don't always land in English.

### "细水长流" (stream flows slow and steady)
❌ A stream that flows slow and steady. (English reader: "OK, a stream. What about love?")
✅ A love that grows quietly over time.
✅ A love that runs deep and steady.

**Why it fails:** Chinese readers know 细水长流 = enduring steady love. English readers see a literal stream and miss the metaphor entirely.

### "满眼藏不住的爱" (love fills eyes, cannot hide)
❌ A love that fills my eyes and cannot be hidden. (unnatural in English)
✅ A love that shines through my eyes and cannot be hidden.

**Why it fails:** "Love fills my eyes" is not an English idiom. "Shines through" captures the visual while reading naturally.

### "山水也未必重逢" (mountains and rivers may not meet again)
❌ Mountains and rivers may never meet again. (English reader: "They never met before either?")
✅ The paths we leave behind may never cross again.
✅ Some distances, once traveled, are never crossed again.

**Why it fails:** 山水 is a cultural shorthand for "the landscape that separates people." English needs the human element (paths, distances, journeys).

---

## 3. Register Mismatch

### "bare" in love poetry
⚠️ lay my best self bare before you (slightly too intense/exposing)
✅ I want to show you the very best of myself.
✅ I want to give you all that I am.

**Rule:** "Bare" connotes vulnerability/exposure. Fine for confessional poetry, too strong for tender love prose.

---

## 4. Missing Cultural Context

### "分享欲" (desire to share)
❌ A desire to share that cannot be stopped. (sounds like sharing objects)
✅ An endless urge to share every little thing with you.
✅ A constant need to tell you everything about my day.

**Rule:** 分享欲 in Chinese youth culture = "I want to tell you everything that happens to me." Not about sharing possessions — about sharing moments.

---

## 5. Idiom Replacement

### "the only one" vs "my one and only"
❌ You will always be the only one. (grammatical but flat)
✅ You will always be my one and only. (natural English love expression)

**Rule:** When there's a well-known English equivalent idiom, use it. Don't translate the Chinese words individually.

---

## 6. Parallel Structure in English

Chinese loves repetition patterns (是...是...是...). English needs variety.

❌ It is X. It is Y. It is Z. (monotonous)
✅ It is X — Y, Z. (varied rhythm)

**Rule:** When Chinese uses 3+ parallel 是/有 structures, break the pattern in English. Alternate sentence shapes.
